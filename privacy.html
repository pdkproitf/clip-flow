1. Who We Are

ClipFlowApp is a video scheduling and publishing tool for content creators. It is operated by its developer. If you have any questions about this policy or how your data is handled, please reach out at pdkitf@gmail.com.

2. What the App Does

Once you create a ClipFlowApp account, you can:

  • Import videos from Google Drive into ClipFlowApp for processing.
  • Split and prepare your video into shorter clips automatically.
  • Schedule publishing — pick a date and time, and ClipFlowApp will post each clip for you.
  • Connect social accounts — link your own Facebook Page or TikTok profile so ClipFlowApp can publish on your behalf.
  • Revoke access — disconnect any social account from ClipFlowApp at any time.

ClipFlowApp only ever posts to accounts you have personally connected. It cannot access, view, or post to anyone else's accounts.

3. What Information We Collect

Account Information

To use ClipFlowApp you must log in. We store your email address and a secure password hash (we never store your password in plain text).

Social Account Connection

When you choose to connect a Facebook Page or TikTok profile, the respective platform grants ClipFlowApp a secure connection credential. We store:

  • A connection credential that lets ClipFlowApp publish on your behalf.
  • A renewal credential used to keep the connection active without asking you to log in again.
  • Your display name and a platform-issued account identifier — so you can see which account is connected in the ClipFlowApp interface.

These credentials are stored in encrypted form and are used only to publish videos you have explicitly scheduled.

Video Files

Videos you import are temporarily stored on our servers while they are being processed and published. Once all scheduled clips for a video have been successfully published, the video file is automatically removed from our servers. We do not retain video content long-term.

Publishing Records

We keep a record of each publish attempt — the scheduled time, whether it succeeded, and any error message if it failed. This lets you track the status of your posts inside ClipFlowApp.

We do not collect your Facebook friends list, your messages, your profile photo, your followers, your TikTok feed, or any other personal profile data beyond what is listed above.

5. Facebook Permissions We Request

ClipFlowApp requests the following Facebook permissions during app review. Each permission has a single, specific purpose:

pages_manage_posts Publish video Reels to your Facebook Page on the schedule you set.
pages_read_engagement Confirm that a post published successfully and retrieve the post link.
pages_show_list List the Pages you manage so you can choose which one to connect to ClipFlowApp.
pages_read_user_content Verify the status of published content on your Page.

When ClipFlowApp reads engagement data from your Page (such as whether a post is live), this is processed under the Page Insights Controller Addendum between you, ClipFlowApp, and Meta. For more information about how Meta processes this data, see Meta's Privacy Policy.

Our guarantee: ClipFlowApp will never read your personal Facebook profile, messages, friends list, or any data from Pages other than the one you connect.

6.1 TikTok API Scopes Used

The following table lists every TikTok API scope ClipFlowApp requests, the specific API endpoint it unlocks, and exactly how ClipFlowApp uses it:

ScopeAPI usedHow ClipFlowApp uses it
video.upload POST /v2/post/publish/video/init/ & chunked PUT upload Initialises a Direct Post on TikTok and uploads the video bytes for a scheduled clip.
video.publish POST /v2/post/publish/status/fetch/ Polls the publish status after upload and retrieves the public post ID once the video is live.
user.info.basic GET /v2/user/info/ (fields: open_id, display_name) Fetches your TikTok display name and account identifier at connect-time so you can see which account is linked in ClipFlowApp.

6.2 TikTok Permissions We Request

ClipFlowApp requests the following TikTok scopes during app review:

video.upload Send your video file to TikTok's servers in preparation for publishing.
video.publish Publish the uploaded video to your TikTok profile at the scheduled time.
user.info.basic Retrieve your TikTok display name and account identifier so you can see which account is connected in ClipFlowApp.

Your consent, your content: Before any video is published to TikTok, you set the schedule and review the content. ClipFlowApp posts only what you have prepared and approved. We never add watermarks, logos, or promotional content to your videos. We never post to another user's TikTok account.

No other TikTok scopes are requested or used. ClipFlowApp does not read your followers, feed, messages, analytics, or any other TikTok data.

7. Who We Share Data With

We do not sell, rent, or share your personal data. The only parties who receive any data from ClipFlowApp are:

  • Meta (Facebook) — ClipFlowApp calls Facebook's API on your behalf. Your connection credential is sent directly to Facebook to authenticate the publish request. No other data about you is sent to Facebook beyond what is required for the API call.
  • TikTok — ClipFlowApp calls TikTok's API on your behalf. Your video file and connection credential are sent to TikTok to complete the publish. No other data about you is sent to TikTok beyond what is required for the API call.

We do not use advertising networks, analytics trackers, or data brokers. No third party receives your credentials, your video content, or your account data for any purpose other than completing the publish you requested.

9. Your Controls — Connect, Disconnect, Delete

Connect or Disconnect a Social Account

Inside ClipFlowApp, you can connect or disconnect your Facebook Page or TikTok profile at any time from your account settings. Disconnecting immediately removes the stored connection credentials from our database and stops all scheduled posts to that account.

Revoke Directly on Facebook

You can also revoke ClipFlowApp's access from Facebook directly: go to Facebook Settings → Security and Login → Apps and Websites, find ClipFlowApp, and remove it. This immediately cancels the connection on Facebook's side.

Revoke Directly on TikTok

You can revoke ClipFlowApp's access from TikTok directly: go to TikTok Settings → Privacy → Connected Apps, find ClipFlowApp, and revoke access.

Delete Your ClipFlowApp Account

To delete your ClipFlowApp account and all associated data, you can do so from your account settings. This action is irreversible and will remove all your data from our systems.

10. How We Protect Your Data

We take reasonable steps to protect your data:

  • Social account credentials are stored using industry-standard encryption — even if our database were accessed by an unauthorised party, the credentials would be unreadable.
  • All connections between your browser and ClipFlowApp use HTTPS (encrypted in transit).
  • Access to production systems is restricted to authorised personnel only.
